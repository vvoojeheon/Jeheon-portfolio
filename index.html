About

My research asks why cities with similar opportunity structures produce different choices, trajectories, and outcomes. I am particularly interested in the mismatch between potential and actual selection within interconnected urban systems.

I approach urban questions through the relationship between structure, selection, and policy. Rather than treating regional decline as a simple population problem, I examine how networked opportunity is unevenly absorbed across places.

My long-term goal is to build rigorous, policy-relevant research on how megaregional integration, institutional arrangements, and infrastructure investment redistribute externalities among cities.
